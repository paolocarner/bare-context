- Compliance as a growth enabler, not a checkbox (ISO 27001, SOC 2, NIS2, DORA, GDPR)

## Buyer Personas & Messaging

Use these when writing LinkedIn content, proposals, outreach, or tool copy.

### Founder / CEO
**Pain:** Liability exposure and reputational risk. Worries about being personally responsible if something goes wrong.  
**Trigger:** "What's our actual exposure here?" / Customer asking for security questionnaire answers.  
**Message:** BARE turns vague cyber risk into a boardroom-ready risk register with financial figures. You make informed decisions, not blind ones.

### Head of Engineering
**Pain:** Feature velocity. Security feels like it slows the team down.  
**Trigger:** "We need to ship, and compliance is blocking us."  
**Message:** BARE integrates security into your SDLC and DevOps workflow — it doesn't gate it. We help you ship faster with fewer surprises.

### CTO
**Pain:** Career risk. One incident and it's their name in the post-mortem.  
**Trigger:** Board asking about security posture. Enterprise customer doing vendor risk assessment.  
**Message:** BARE gives you the credible, documented security programme that lets you walk into any boardroom or customer audit with confidence.

### Head of Sales / VP Sales
**Pain:** Deals stalling or dying because of security questionnaires and vendor risk processes.  
**Trigger:** "We lost another deal because we couldn't answer their security questions."  
**Message:** BARE turns your security programme into a sales asset. Certification and documentation that shortens sales cycles and unlocks enterprise accounts.

### CFO / Finance Director *(influencer — typically supports the CEO on security budget decisions; does not own the line directly)*
**Pain:** Can't evaluate whether cybersecurity spend is proportionate to the risk carried, and can't give the CEO, board, or auditors a defensible answer when asked. Heat maps and red/amber/green ratings don't translate into the financial language the rest of the business runs on.  
**Trigger:** Cyber insurance renewal or premium increase. Board asking about security spend justification. Audit committee reporting. A specific budget request from the security function the CFO can't independently evaluate.  
**Message:** The heat maps your security team uses can't answer what matters most — are we spending the right amount for the risk we carry? There are no silver bullets for quantifying risk, but FAIR is a much more defensible way to stand behind the euros spent on security. It shows your risk exposure in financial terms, and how much it drops when you invest in the measures on the table — the same expected-loss logic your insurer already uses.

## Content & Messaging Principles

**BARE does NOT:**
- Use fear, uncertainty, or doubt (FUD) to sell
- Push vendor products or earn referral fees
- Claim to "manage" client security (advisory only — clients own their risk)
- Overpromise on outcomes

**BARE DOES:**
- Lead with evidence and data (FAIR, ENISA statistics, real incident costs)
- Frame security as a business enabler and growth asset
- Speak to business objectives, not just technical controls
- Acknowledge complexity and nuance — no silver bullets

**LinkedIn voice:** Paolo posts in first person, European business English, occasional Italian cultural reference. Thoughtful, slightly contrarian (challenges industry FUD), professionally warm. Posts often start with a specific observation or data point, not a generic hook.

## Regulatory Context (European)

BARE operates primarily in these frameworks — assume familiarity:
- **ISO 27001** — Information security management systems (certification path)
- **SOC 2** — US-origin but required by many SaaS enterprise clients
- **NIS2** — EU directive, essential/important entity classification, supply chain obligations
- **DORA** — Financial sector digital operational resilience (ICT risk, third-party)
- **GDPR** — Data protection baseline across all EU clients
- **CRA** — Cyber Resilience Act (emerging, product security, OSS implications)
- **Dutch specifics:** AVG (GDPR NL), Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens, ZZP/freelancer regulations
